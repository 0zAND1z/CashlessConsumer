UPI Setup

1. How can I use #UPI?

UPI needs an Android phone (iOS support coming soon), mobile data / WiFi , Mobile number registered with bank and a PSP application (Payment Service Provider, which can you be any bank / company) installed and setup on your phone.

2. Getting started

Check if your bank supports UPI, Install a UPI app, setup your VPA(Virtual Payment address) and link your bank accounts, setup M-PIN, transact.

3. Supported banks and apps

Check if your bank is offering UPI support from list maintained by NPCI (UPI operator). Pick and install any app from UPI Apps. If you are confused, check app comparison for help.

4. Register in app

The app would register you by sending an SMS, choose the SIM having mobile linked to your bank account. Provide basic profile details, setup app lock password.

5. Setup VPA, Link bank account

VPA is Virtual Payment Address, similar to email address. This is what is needed to send / receive money. You no longer need to share bank account number, IFSC code, mobile number etc. Create your VPA. Select the bank in which you have an account, it would list accounts registered to your mobile number. Link it to the VPA.

6. Send / Receive money from your bank accounts to others

To receive money, you just need to share your VPA to other person who is sending money. To send money, you also need to setup M-PIN, similar to your ATM PIN. If you know your M-PIN, you can send money to other VPA, Bank account + IFSC code. You can generate your M-PIN using the option in the app if you have debit card associated with the bank account. Chose generate M-PIN, enter last 6 digits of debit card, one time password you will get and then set you M-PIN.

Note :- Never share your M-PIN with anyone. Its just like your ATM M-PIN. Remember to block your SIM card, if you happen to loose SIM card, debit card together.

7. QR Codes

Since typing VPA to transfer money can be time consuming, you can generate your QRCode for any VPA, any person can scan your QRCode and it would directly open payments screen on their UPI app. They would still need to enter amount and M-PIN to complete the transaction.
